Pink Figurehead - a logarithmic function

Transformations and Modelling 🌸

I took this photo in a makeup shop in Sunway Pyramid. I chose this photo because this shop is one of my favourite makeup stores in Malaysia.

To model the pink figurehead appearing in the photo, I selected the parent function:

\[ y=\log(x) \]

From observing the orientation of the curve, I noticed that the graph was decreasing from left to right. Therefore, a reflection across the y-axis was needed.

\[ y=\log(-x) \]

I also applied a vertical compression using the coefficient 0.1, which made the graph flatter and matched the image more closely.

After applying translations, the final function became:

\[ y=0.1\log(-(x-0.6))+1.2 \]

Summary of Transformations 🍞
  • Reflection across the y-axis
  • Vertical compression by factor 0.1
  • Horizontal translation 0.6 units right
  • Vertical translation 1.2 units upward

The roof - Quadratic Reciprocal Function ✨

Transformations and Modelling 🌸

I took this photo at a park in Malaysia. This photo is very memorable and important to me because my family came to Malaysia to visit me, and we spent time walking around together. Whenever I look at this photo, many memories come flooding back.

To model the roof appearing in the photograph, I began by identifying that the shape closely resembled a quadratic reciprocal function. Therefore, I selected the parent function:

\[ y=\frac{1}{x^2} \]

as the starting point for my model.

From observing the curve in the image, I noticed that the graph had a similar general shape to the reciprocal function, with two curves approaching a vertical asymptote. However, the graph in the photograph appeared much wider than the parent function, which indicated that a vertical compression was required. I adjusted the function multiple times until the width of the curve closely matched the roof in the image.

Initially, I found it difficult to determine the correct compression ratio and compare the shapes accurately. To simplify the process, I focused only on the shape of the graph before applying translations. While comparing the graphs, I noticed how quickly the edges moved away from the asymptotes. From this, I determined that the graph required a significant vertical compression, and through observation and repeated adjustments, I found that the factor:

\[ \frac{1.8}{100} \]

provided the best fit.

I also noticed that the graph did not approach the x-axis directly. Instead, the addition of a constant term in the denominator adjusted the shape of the curve. Therefore, I added 3 to the denominator, which widened the graph and prevented the function from becoming undefined near the translated asymptote.

After determining the shape, I applied translations to return the graph to the correct position in the photograph. I shifted the graph 0.6 units to the right, which resulted in:

\[ (x-0.6)^2 \]

I then translated the graph 1.5 units upward so that the curve passed through the correct region of the roof.

This resulted in the final function:

\[ y=\frac{1.8}{100(x-0.6)^2+3}+1.5 \]

which closely models the shape shown in the photograph.

Summary of Transformations 🍞
  • Vertical compression: \[ \frac{1}{x^2}\rightarrow\frac{1.8}{x^2} \]
  • Horizontal compression caused by the factor of 100: \[ \frac{1.8}{x^2}\rightarrow\frac{1.8}{100x^2} \]
  • Addition of +3 in the denominator: \[ \frac{1.8}{100x^2}\rightarrow\frac{1.8}{100x^2+3} \]
  • Horizontal translation 0.6 units to the right: \[ \frac{1.8}{100x^2+3}\rightarrow \frac{1.8}{100(x-0.6)^2+3} \]
  • Vertical translation 1.5 units upward: \[ \frac{1.8}{100(x-0.6)^2+3} \rightarrow \frac{1.8}{100(x-0.6)^2+3}+1.5 \]
Domain and Range ✨

The restricted domain of my function is:

\[ 0.367\le x\le0.85 \]

I limited the domain to the visible portion of the roof shown in the photograph. If I had taken the photo from a closer distance, the roof would have appeared larger in the image and the graph may have fitted the shape more accurately. However, because the roof appeared relatively small in the photograph, the model is only valid for the visible section captured in the image.

The parent function:

\[ y=\frac{1}{x^2} \]

has the domain:

\[ D:\{x\in\mathbb{R}\mid x\ne0\} \]

and the range:

\[ R:\{y\in\mathbb{R}\mid y>0\} \]

This means the function is defined for all real values except:

\[ x=0 \]

where the function has a vertical asymptote.

After applying the transformations, my function also contains a vertical asymptote. However, because I restricted the domain to:

\[ 0.367\le x\le0.85 \]

the interval lies completely away from the asymptote. Therefore, the undefined behaviour does not affect the visible section of my model.

Since the function is continuous on the restricted interval, the range can be determined by evaluating the function at both endpoints of the domain.

After evaluating the function at:

\[ x=0.367 \quad \text{and} \quad x=0.85 \]

I obtained approximate output values of 1.71 and 1.695.

Therefore, the approximate range of the function is:

\[ R:\{y\in\mathbb{R}\mid 1.695\le y\le1.71\} \]

Memorable book - trigonometric function with an asymptote

Transformations and Modelling 🌸

I chose this photo because it reminds me of my experience visiting bookstores and cafes with my friends in Malaysia. In these places, I often discover interesting books and artistic designs. Malaysia has many locations that combine modern and traditional art styles, and I was attracted to the unique wave pattern on this book cover when I saw it.

The repeating curves immediately caught my attention because they looked similar to a trigonometric graph. Since mathematics can be used to model patterns found in everyday life, I decided to analyze the shape more carefully.

After observing the pattern, I noticed that it closely resembled a cosecant graph because of its repeating curved sections and asymptotic behaviour. Therefore, I selected the parent function:

$$y=\csc(x)$$

as the starting point for my model.

My final function is:

$$y=-10.6\csc(-0.5(x+1.27))+2.3$$

From observing the orientation of the curves in the image, I noticed that the graph opened downward rather than upward. This showed that a reflection across the x-axis was needed. I also applied a vertical stretch so that the height of the arches matched the wave pattern on the book cover.

At first, it was difficult to determine the correct period because the repeating sections were close together in the image. To solve this, I measured the horizontal distance between two repeating curves. Then, I adjusted the value of k in Desmos until the period of the graph matched the pattern in the photograph.

After the repeating shape matched the image, I applied horizontal and vertical translations to position the graph correctly over the book cover. I also restricted the domain so that only the visible section of the pattern appeared. As a result, the function closely models the decorative wave design shown in the photograph.

Summary of Transformations 🍞

For the function:

$$y=-10.6\csc(-0.5(x+1.27))+2.3$$

the transformations are:

  • Reflection across the x-axis because of −10.6
  • Reflection across the y-axis because of −0.5
  • Vertical stretch by a factor of 10.6
  • Horizontal stretch by a factor of 2
  • Horizontal translation 1.27 units to the left
  • Vertical translation 2.3 units upward
  • Restricted domain to fit the visible section of the pattern
Domain and Range ✨

The parent function is:

$$y=\csc(x)$$

The domain of the parent cosecant function is:

$$D:\{x\in\mathbb{R}\mid x\ne n\pi,\ n\in\mathbb{Z}\}$$

This is because cosecant is undefined when $\sin(x)=0$. These undefined values create vertical asymptotes.

The range of the parent function is:

$$R:\{y\in\mathbb{R}\mid y\le -1 \text{ or } y\ge 1\}$$

The value $x\approx11.30$ is excluded because the graph has a vertical asymptote there.

After the transformations, the range becomes:

$$R:\{y\in\mathbb{R}\mid y\le-8.3 \text{ or } y\ge12.9\}$$

This range shows that the graph still has two separated parts, which is a key feature of the cosecant function.
